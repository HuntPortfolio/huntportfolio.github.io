Apocalyptic, in a Sense.

by nathan hunt

    I once dreamt about being an astronaut. I dreamt of gripping my seat as my ship rumbled through the atmosphere during take-off. I dreamt of slowly drifting through the endless, inky void. I dreamt of being a world away, experiencing new sights, scents, textures, echoes, rattles. I dreamt of something better, a place to be.

    I imagined landing on a distant planet, somewhere outside of our solar system. The smooth, cyan ground was covered in a thick red fog. As soon as I stepped out of my ship, I was knocked back by blisteringly cold wind whipping at my helmet. My feet struggled to find grip in the shifting sands. The terrain seemed endless, there were no visible landmarks in any direction. After waiting out the winds, I ventured out to search for something. The end goal wasn't very clear, but I trudged along anyway to try and find some sort of bearing.




Step by Step Guide to Better Technical Writing:

  1. Ye

    Self-expression is not something that comes easily to me, and I find writing about my previous works to be doubly hard. I've been reviewing my three projects and after careful deliberation, I've decided to call them "okay." I can't call my work stellar and I certainly don't consider it anything ground-breaking or even terribly special. However, I do see my skill as a technical writer improving, and more importantly seeing my own work no longer makes me embarrassed for the potential reader.

    A lot of the issues in my works are systemic, and come from a fundamental misunderstanding of the prompt. With Project 1 I spent a sizable amount of time debating how to even begin tackling the prompt. The brainstorming and writing processes entwined themselves into a nasty vortex in which nothing escaped; in the end I had nothing. My writing background is almost entirely in Engfish and is a product of the Massachusetts Public School System; my teachers would be ashamed to hear me say it, but it's an undeniable truth. Adapting to this class required re-learning how to write clearly and succinctly, the fluff would have to go.
